What Working in the Kitchen Taught Me While On My Cybersecurity Journey

Nov. 25, 2024 read Cybersecurity

My time working in a fast-paced kitchen has taught me skills I never expected to carry over into my cybersecurity journey. While I’m still looking to land my first role in the field, I’ve realized that the lessons from the kitchen, preparation, precision, and adaptability, are just as valuable in cybersecurity. Both worlds demand a sharp focus on problem-solving, teamwork, and continuous improvement.

Preparation is Key: Laying the Groundwork

In the kitchen, "mise en place" (everything in its place) is essential. Prepping ingredients, organizing tools, and setting up your station ensures service runs smoothly. In cybersecurity, preparation is just as important. From understanding frameworks to practicing hands-on labs, success often hinges on having everything in place ahead of time. Whether it’s avoiding a botched recipe or preparing for a job interview, preparation makes all the difference.

Recipes and Playbooks: Clear Roadmaps

A recipe offers clear, step-by-step instructions to create a dish, while a cybersecurity playbook provides a structured guide to handle incidents like malware or phishing attacks. Both require clarity and attention to detail. Whether perfecting a dish or responding to a hypothetical cyber threat, following a well-designed plan helps achieve consistent, successful outcomes.

Timing and Execution: Every Second Counts

In the kitchen, timing is everything. Overcook a dish, and it’s ruined. In cybersecurity, responding quickly and efficiently to challenges, like timed labs or interviews, is just as important. Both fields demand quick, focused execution. Knowing how to act under pressure and within tight timeframes is a skill I’ve honed in the kitchen and continue to apply to my cybersecurity training.

Adapting to the Unexpected

No matter how well-prepared you are, things go wrong. In the kitchen, ingredients might run out, orders might get mixed up, or dishes might not turn out as planned. In cybersecurity labs, unexpected errors, misconfigurations, or challenges are just as common. Learning to adapt, troubleshoot, and think on my feet has been a skill I developed behind the stove and one I carry into every lab and learning opportunity.

Teamwork: The Core Ingredient

In a kitchen, everyone has a role to play, from chefs to servers, all working together to create a great experience. I’ve seen how vital teamwork is, whether it’s learning from others in my cybersecurity course, sharing ideas, or contributing to group projects. Success in both worlds depends on trust, communication, and understanding how to work as part of a team.

Attention to Detail: The Small Things Matter

A missing ingredient or a slight misstep in a recipe can lead to failure, just like overlooking details in a cybersecurity lab can throw off results. The kitchen taught me to triple-check my work, a habit I’ve carried into my cybersecurity journey. Precision and attention to detail are non-negotiable, whether it’s crafting a dish or configuring system settings.

Continuous Learning: Always Improving

Both fields require a commitment to growth. In the kitchen, there’s always a new technique to learn or a dish to refine. In cybersecurity, I’m constantly learning new tools, frameworks, and approaches through labs and study. Continuous learning drives me forward as I prepare for opportunities in the field.

Although I haven’t started my cybersecurity career yet, the kitchen has prepared me in ways I never expected. It taught me resilience, adaptability, and the importance of preparation, all of which have shaped my approach to this new path. These experiences remind me that skills from one field can translate into unexpected areas. Whether crafting a perfect dish or preparing for a cybersecurity role, the principles are the same, prepare well, adapt when needed, work as a team, and never stop learning. I’m excited to bring these lessons to my future in cybersecurity.
